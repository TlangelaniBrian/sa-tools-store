✅ Good News — You Qualify as an EME!

Exempted Micro Enterprises (annual turnover under R10 million) automatically qualify as B-BBEE Level 4 contributors, regardless of ownership structure — unless the entity is 51% or more black-owned (Level 2) or 100% black-owned (Level 1).

  • If 51%–99% black-owned: automatic Level 2 (125% procurement recognition)
  • If 100% black-owned: automatic Level 1 (135% procurement recognition)
  • Otherwise: automatic Level 4 (100% procurement recognition)

Affidavit & Documentation
Sworn Affidavit from SAPS Commissioner or Notary
An EME affidavit must be commissioned by a South African Police Service commissioner of oaths, a notary public, or a registered auditor. It must confirm annual turnover below R10 million and the ownership percentage of black persons.
Evidence: Original commissioned affidavit, signed and stamped within the last 12 months
✓
Required
Confirmation of Annual Turnover Below R10 Million
The affidavit must clearly state that your business's total annual revenue from all sources does not exceed R10 million for the most recent financial year.
Evidence: Most recent annual financial statements or management accounts. Turnover figure must match affidavit.
✓
Required
Business Registration Documents
Certified copies of the company's registration documents (CIPC) or, for sole traders, a valid business licence or municipal certificate.
Evidence: CIPC registration certificate (CoR14.3), partnership agreement, or sole trader business licence
✓
Required
Black Ownership Declaration (if claiming Level 1 or 2)
If your business is 51%+ black-owned, the affidavit must state the exact percentage of black ownership to claim Level 2 or Level 1 status. Certified IDs of black owners may be required by clients.
Evidence: Certified copies of South African IDs of black owners, shareholding register or partnership agreement showing % ownership
✓
If applicable
Valid SARS Tax Clearance / Good Standing Certificate
Many clients require a valid Tax Clearance Certificate (TCC) or SARS PIN confirming good standing, even from EMEs. Obtain via eFiling or SARS branch.
Evidence: SARS TCC or Good Standing Certificate (not older than 12 months), or SARS eFiling PIN
✓
Recommended
Annual Affidavit Renewal
The EME affidavit must be renewed each financial year. Ensure you have a system in place to renew before expiry to avoid losing compliant status with clients.
Evidence: Calendar reminder or compliance calendar showing next renewal date. Affidavit date should not exceed 12 months.

B-BBEE Certificate Issued by Verification Agency (Optional)
While not required for EMEs, some large clients and government entities prefer a formal B-BBEE certificate issued by an accredited verification agency. This costs more but removes any affidavit queries.
Evidence: B-BBEE verification certificate from SANAS-accredited agency, valid for 12 months

Register on the National Treasury's Central Supplier Database (CSD) to be eligible for government contracts. Your B-BBEE affidavit information is captured here.

Voting Rights
Black Voting Rights ≥ 25%
At least 25% of exercisable voting rights in the enterprise must be held by black persons as defined in the B-BBEE Act.

Ownership Fulfilment
Realisation Points — Ownership Fulfilment ≥ 25%
Measures the extent to which ownership obligations have been met without using third-party financing (i.e., "modified flow-through" principle). Tracks how much of the ownership is "real" and unencumbered.
Evidence: Loan agreements, sale-of-shares agreements, vendor finance arrangements, audited equity structures

The net value of black ownership (shares net of debt used to acquire them) should be increasing year-on-year, indicating genuine economic empowerment progress.
Evidence: Annual B-BBEE ownership schedule from auditor; loan amortisation schedules

Maximum: 25 points

Training Spend
Skills Spend ≥ 2% of Leviable Amount on Black Employees
At least 2% of the total annual wage bill (leviable amount) must be spent on training and skills development for black employees. Spend on bursaries, learnerships, courses, and skills programmes qualifies.
Evidence: Training invoices, proof of payment, learnership agreements, bursary agreements, WSP/ATR submitted to SETA
8
pts
Skills Spend on Black Women ≥ 1% of Leviable Amount
At least 1% of the leviable amount directed to training specifically for black women employees.
Evidence: Training records segregated by gender; invoices; learnership enrolment forms
4
pts
Learnerships & Programmes
Black Learnership / Apprenticeship / Internship Participants
Hosting or funding at least one registered learnership, apprenticeship, or workplace-based internship for black learners qualifies for points. NQF-registered programmes score highest.
Evidence: Signed learnership agreements with SETA, attendance registers, NQF registration confirmation
5
pts
WSP & ATR Submitted to Relevant SETA
The Workplace Skills Plan (WSP) and Annual Training Report (ATR) must be submitted to your SETA before 30 June each year. Failure to submit disqualifies skills spend points.
Evidence: SETA acknowledgement / confirmation of WSP/ATR submission; SETA grant approval letter
4
pts
Bursaries / Scholarships for Black Students
Financial contributions to black students at registered tertiary institutions (universities, TVET colleges) qualify as skills spend.
Evidence: Bursary agreements, university registration letters, proof of payment, student ID
4
pts

Socio-Economic Development (SED)
Maximum: 25 points
0 /25 pts ▼
SED Contributions
SED Spend ≥ 1% of Net Profit After Tax (NPAT)
At least 1% of your NPAT must be contributed to qualifying SED beneficiaries: black people, organisations serving black communities, or programmes that advance black socio-economic upliftment.
Evidence: Audited NPAT figure; bank statements / EFT proof; beneficiary letters; NPO registration of recipient (if applicable)
12
pts
Beneficiaries Are Black Persons or Black-Serving Organisations
Contributions must flow to black beneficiaries. Qualifying recipients include black-owned SMMEs, registered NPOs/NGOs primarily serving black communities, schools in previously disadvantaged areas, and bursary funds.
Evidence: Beneficiary profile/description, NPO registration certificate, confirmation of black beneficiary demographics
5
pts
SED Contribution Documentation and Receipts
All SED contributions must be supported by proper documentation. The verification agency will require written agreements or letters from beneficiaries confirming receipt and purpose of funds.
Evidence: Section 18A certificates (if donation), beneficiary acknowledgement letters, EFT proof of payment, board resolution authorising contribution
4
pts
SED Recorded in Financial Statements
SED contributions must appear as a line item in the annual financial statements or be evidenced in a management accounts schedule for the financial year being measured.
Evidence: Annual financial statements noting SED expense; management account with SED line; auditor confirmation

⚡ Priority Actions to Improve Your Score

    ℹ Generic Enterprise Scorecard

    Generic enterprises (turnover above R50 million) are subject to the full B-BBEE scorecard with 5 elements: Ownership, Management Control, Skills Development, Enterprise & Supplier Development, and Socio-Economic Development. They also face Priority Elements with sub-minimum thresholds.

    • Maximum scorecard points: 109 points (including bonus points)
    • Priority Elements: Ownership, Skills Development, Enterprise & Supplier Development
    • Sub-minimum thresholds apply — failure triggers an automatic one-level discount
    • Generic enterprises must obtain verification from a SANAS-accredited agency
    • Strongly recommended: engage a specialist B-BBEE consultant for Generic scorecards
