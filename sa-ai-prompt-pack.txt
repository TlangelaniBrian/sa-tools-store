SECTION 1: CLIENT COMMUNICATION
================================================================================
Professional, SA-contextualised client correspondence — quotations, invoices,
scope of work & payment follow-ups.
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

[01] PROFESSIONAL QUOTATION EMAIL

Write a professional quotation email for a South African business. The email is
from [YOUR BUSINESS NAME], a [TYPE OF BUSINESS, e.g. graphic design studio]
based in [CITY]. The client is [CLIENT NAME / COMPANY]. The quoted service is
[DESCRIPTION OF SERVICE] at a total cost of [AMOUNT IN RANDS] (VAT [inclusive /
exclusive]). Quote validity is [NUMBER] days. Payment terms are [e.g. 50%
upfront, 50% on delivery]. Use a warm but professional South African business
tone. Include a clear breakdown line, a VAT note if applicable, and a polite
call to action to accept the quote.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

[02] INVOICE FOLLOW-UP EMAIL — FIRST REMINDER

Write a polite first-reminder email to a client whose invoice is [NUMBER] days
overdue. Business name: [YOUR BUSINESS NAME]. Client name: [CLIENT NAME].
Invoice number: [INVOICE NUMBER]. Amount outstanding: R[AMOUNT]. Original due
date: [DATE]. Payment reference: [REFERENCE]. Banking details: [BANK, ACCOUNT
NAME, ACCOUNT NUMBER, BRANCH CODE]. Tone should be friendly and assume the
non-payment was an oversight. End with a clear call to action and offer to
resend the invoice if needed.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

[03] INVOICE FOLLOW-UP EMAIL — FINAL DEMAND

Write a firm but professional final-demand letter for an overdue invoice in a
South African context. The amount owed is R[AMOUNT] on invoice [INVOICE NUMBER],
now [NUMBER] days overdue. State that if payment is not received within [NUMBER,
e.g. 5] business days, the matter will be referred to a debt collector and/or
the business reserves the right to charge interest at the prescribed legal rate
under the National Credit Act. Keep the tone firm and professional. Include
banking details: [BANKING DETAILS].

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

[04] SCOPE OF WORK EMAIL

[LIST EXCLUSIONS]. Revision rounds included: [NUMBER]. Total fee: R[AMOUNT].
Payment schedule: [PAYMENT TERMS]. The email should be structured with clear
headings, confirm mutual understanding, and ask the client to reply with written
approval to proceed.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

that this additional work will require a separate quotation of approximately
R[AMOUNT]. Keep the tone respectful and solution-focused — the goal is to
preserve the relationship while protecting the business from unpaid work. Offer
to send a new quotation promptly if the client wishes to proceed.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

[06] PROJECT DELAY NOTIFICATION TO CLIENT

Apologise sincerely without making excessive excuses, explain the revised
timeline clearly, and reassure the client of your commitment to quality
delivery. If the delay is partly the client's fault (e.g. late feedback),
mention this diplomatically.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

================================================================================
Protection of Personal Information Act — privacy policy, data breach notices,
consent forms & staff data-handling policy.
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

[11] BUSINESS PRIVACY POLICY (POPIA-COMPLIANT)

Write a POPIA-compliant privacy policy for a South African [TYPE OF BUSINESS,
e.g. online retail store / consulting firm / medical practice]. Business name:
[BUSINESS NAME]. Registration number: [REG NUMBER]. Information Officer: [NAME
AND EMAIL]. Website: [WEBSITE URL]. Types of personal information collected:
[e.g. name, email, ID number, banking details, health information]. Purpose for
collection: [e.g. billing, service delivery, marketing with consent]. Third
parties data is shared with: [e.g. payment processors, accountants]. Include
sections on data subject rights, retention periods, how to lodge a complaint
with the Information Regulator, and cookie policy where applicable. Write in
plain English.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

[12] DATA BREACH NOTIFICATION TO AFFECTED PARTIES

Write a POPIA-compliant data breach notification letter to affected data
subjects. The breach occurred at [BUSINESS NAME] on or around [DATE DISCOVERED].
Nature of the breach: [DESCRIBE WHAT HAPPENED, e.g. unauthorised access to a
database containing email addresses and phone numbers]. Information compromised:
[LIST DATA TYPES EXPOSED]. Steps taken to contain the breach: [ACTIONS TAKEN].
Recommended actions for affected individuals: [e.g. change passwords, monitor
bank accounts]. Contact for queries: [NAME, EMAIL, PHONE]. The letter should be
clear, factual, and reassuring — and note that the Information Regulator of
South Africa has been notified as required under Section 22 of POPIA.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

party: [BUSINESS NAME]. Information Officer: [NAME]. Registration number: [REG
NUMBER]. Date and time of breach: [DATE / APPROXIMATE TIME]. Description of the
incident: [DETAILED DESCRIPTION]. Categories of data subjects affected: [e.g.
customers, employees]. Approximate number of affected persons: [NUMBER].
Mitigation steps taken: [ACTIONS]. Format it as a formal letter with all
required POPIA Section 22 fields addressed.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

[14] CUSTOMER CONSENT FORM (MARKETING & COMMUNICATIONS)

obtain customer consent for marketing communications. Business: [BUSINESS NAME].

be specific, informed, and freely given as required by POPIA. Include a clear
opt-out mechanism and state how data will be stored and protected. Write both a
long-form version (for a website or printed form) and a short-form version (for
a WhatsApp or SMS double-opt-in message).

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

[18] THIRD-PARTY PROCESSOR DATA SHARING AGREEMENT CLAUSE

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

[20] WEBSITE COOKIE NOTICE (POPIA-ALIGNED)

Write a POPIA-aligned cookie notice and consent banner text for a South African
