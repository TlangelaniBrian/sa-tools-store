Independent Contractor Service Agreement
This Agreement is entered into on [DD Month YYYY] between:

Service Provider ("Contractor"): [YOUR FULL NAME / ENTITY NAME], identity number / registration number [ID/Reg No], residing at / registered at [Your Address].

Client ("Client"): [CLIENT COMPANY / FULL NAME], registration number [Reg No], represented by [AUTHORISED SIGNATORY NAME] in the capacity of [TITLE], with principal place of business at [Client Address].

Together referred to as "the Parties."

1. Appointment & Nature of Relationship

The Client appoints the Contractor as an independent service provider, and the Contractor accepts such appointment, on the terms set out herein. The Contractor is not an employee of the Client. Nothing in this Agreement creates an employment relationship, partnership, joint venture, or agency between the Parties. The Contractor retains the right to provide services to other clients, provided there is no conflict of interest with obligations owed to the Client under this Agreement.

2. Scope of Work & Deliverables

The Contractor will provide the following services: [Describe services clearly — e.g. "Brand identity design including logo, colour palette, typography guide, and business card design in agreed formats"]. The specific deliverables, acceptance criteria, and milestones are set out in the attached Schedule of Work / Statement of Work (SOW). Any work outside the agreed scope will be quoted separately in writing before commencement. The Contractor reserves the right to decline out-of-scope requests.

3. Fees & Payment Terms

The Client will pay the Contractor the fees set out in the agreed Quotation / Schedule of Fees, which forms part of this Agreement. Unless otherwise agreed in writing: (a) a deposit of 50% of the total fee is payable before work commences; (b) the balance is payable within 30 days of final delivery or the date of invoice, whichever is earlier; (c) VAT is payable in addition to the quoted fee where the Contractor is VAT-registered; (d) late payments attract interest at the prime overdraft rate plus 2% per annum, calculated daily and compounded monthly, as permitted under the National Credit Act 34 of 2005; (e) the Contractor may suspend services and withhold deliverables until all outstanding amounts are settled in full.

4. Intellectual Property

All intellectual property created specifically and exclusively for the Client under this Agreement shall vest in the Client upon receipt of full and final payment of all fees and expenses. Until payment is received in full, all rights, title, and interest in all work product remain with the Contractor. The Contractor retains the right to use work created under this Agreement in their portfolio and for self-promotional purposes, unless the Client requests otherwise in writing and the Parties agree on specific non-disclosure terms. Pre-existing intellectual property, tools, frameworks, and know-how owned by the Contractor prior to this engagement remain the exclusive property of the Contractor.

5. Confidentiality

Each Party undertakes to keep confidential all proprietary, commercially sensitive, or business information ("Confidential Information") disclosed by the other Party during the term of this Agreement and for a period of two (2) years after termination. Confidential Information does not include information that: (a) is or becomes publicly known through no breach of this Agreement; (b) was already known to the receiving Party before disclosure; (c) is lawfully obtained from a third party without restriction; or (d) must be disclosed by law or court order. The Contractor may disclose to subcontractors on a need-to-know basis, provided such subcontractors are bound by equivalent confidentiality obligations.

6. Timeline & Force Majeure

The Contractor will use reasonable endeavours to deliver by the agreed dates. Delays caused by the Client (including late provision of content, feedback, or approvals) will extend delivery timelines accordingly. The Contractor is not liable for delays caused by circumstances beyond their reasonable control, including load-shedding, connectivity failures, illness, or other force majeure events, provided the Contractor notifies the Client promptly of any such delay.

7. Cancellation & Termination

Either Party may terminate this Agreement by giving [14 / 30] calendar days' written notice to the other Party. Upon termination: (a) the Client will pay the Contractor for all work completed up to the date of termination, calculated on a pro-rata basis or per agreed milestones; (b) if the Client cancels after work has commenced, the deposit paid is non-refundable and covers work completed to date; (c) if work completed at termination exceeds the deposit, the balance is immediately due; (d) the Contractor will deliver all completed work product to the Client upon receipt of payment for same. Either Party may terminate immediately if the other Party commits a material breach that remains unremedied for 7 days after written notice, or becomes insolvent.

8. Limitation of Liability

To the maximum extent permitted by South African law, the Contractor's total liability to the Client under or in connection with this Agreement shall not exceed the total fees paid by the Client in the three (3) months preceding the event giving rise to the claim. The Contractor is not liable for indirect, consequential, special, or punitive damages, loss of profit, or loss of business opportunity, howsoever arising. The Contractor's obligations under this Agreement are obligations of means, not of result, except where specifically stated.

9. Governing Law & Dispute Resolution

This Agreement is governed by the laws of the Republic of South Africa. In the event of a dispute, the Parties agree to first attempt to resolve the matter through good-faith negotiation within 14 days of written notice. If unresolved, the dispute shall be referred to mediation before any legal proceedings are instituted. The jurisdiction for any court proceedings is the [Gauteng / Western Cape / KwaZulu-Natal] High Court or the appropriate Magistrate's Court, as determined by the quantum of the claim.

10. Entire Agreement

This Agreement, together with any attached Schedule of Work or Quotation, constitutes the entire agreement between the Parties with respect to its subject matter and supersedes all prior negotiations, representations, or agreements. Amendments must be in writing and signed by both Parties. If any provision is found to be unenforceable, the remaining provisions continue in full force and effect.
